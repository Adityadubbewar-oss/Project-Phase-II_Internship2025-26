Frequently Asked Questions

1. What does the "Coalition Strength Score" represent?
The Coalition Strength Score is a composite index calculated by weighing absolute wealth (GDP Per Capita) against structural stability indicators like the Gini Index and Unemployment rate. A higher score indicates a resilient economy with relatively balanced wealth distribution.
2. How is "Income Capability Disparity" determined?
Capability Disparity uses the Gini Index (0-100 scale) as a base. We categorize scores above 50 as 'Extreme Disparity', 40-50 as 'Moderate Dispersion', and below 35 as 'Relatively Balanced'. The dashboard aggregates this score to highlight regions requiring structural intervention.
3. What is the source of this military data?
The current dataset is derived from the primary dataset, which mirrors international defense indicators spanning 2001-2024. It covers 20 major nations analyzed for defense and military capability trends.
4. Why do some countries show 0% for certain military asset distributions?
A 0% value usually denotes a data reporting gap or a lack of granular public defense data for that specific reporting year in the primary dataset. Our analysis treats these as null identifiers during aggregation to maintain accuracy.
